# How to Find a Reliable Electrician in NZ: LBP Checks & Cost Guide

Finding a trustworthy electrician in New Zealand doesn't have to be stressful — but it does require knowing what to look for. Electrical work is heavily regulated in NZ, and hiring the wrong person can put your family's safety at risk, void your home insurance, and cost significantly more to put right.

Here's everything you need to know before you hire.

---

## Why Licensing Matters

In New Zealand, all electrical work on fixed wiring must be carried out by a **Registered Electrical Worker (REW)**, as required under the Electricity Act 1992. This isn't a suggestion — it's the law. Hiring an unregistered person for electrical work is illegal and can:

- Void your home insurance
- Create an uncertifiable fault that must be disclosed when you sell
- Put your household at serious risk of fire or electrocution

---

## How to Check a Licence: The EWRB Register

Before any work begins, check your electrician's registration on the **Electrical Workers Registration Board (EWRB)** website at [ewrb.govt.nz](https://www.ewrb.govt.nz).

You can search by name or registration number. A current, active registration means the person is licensed to carry out electrical work. Take 30 seconds to do this — it's free.

Key licence categories to know:

- **Electrician** — can carry out and certify most residential and commercial electrical work
- **Electrical Inspector** — conducts inspection and testing
- **Line Mechanic** — works on overhead and underground power lines

For home and business jobs, you want a registered **Electrician**.

---

## What Questions Should You Ask?

When contacting electricians, ask:

1. **What is your REW registration number?** — Verify it at ewrb.govt.nz before they start
2. **Will you provide an Electrical Safety Certificate (ESC)?** — Required by law for notifiable electrical work; if they won't, walk away
3. **Are you a member of Master Electricians NZ?** — Not compulsory, but a sign of professional standards
4. **Do you carry public liability insurance?** — Protects you if something is damaged during the job
5. **Can you provide a written quote?** — Get it in writing before work starts

## Average Electrician Costs Across NZ

| Job Type | Estimated Cost (NZD) |
|---|---|
| Call-out / hourly rate | $90 – $280/hr (+ call-out $80–$150) |
| New power outlet | $120 – $300 |
| Smoke alarm installation | $80 – $150 per alarm |
| Switchboard upgrade | $1,500 – $4,000 |
| EV charger installation | $800 – $2,500 |
| Ceiling fan installation | $150 – $400 |
| Full house rewire (3-bed) | $8,000 – $20,000+ |

Prices vary by region — Auckland and Wellington typically sit at the higher end. Always get at least three quotes for jobs over $500.

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- **No REW number or won't provide one** — walk away immediately
- **Quotes cash-only with no invoice** — legitimate electricians provide GST invoices
- **Prices that seem too good to be true** — cheap unlicensed work costs far more to fix
- **Won't provide an Electrical Safety Certificate** — this is a legal requirement for notifiable work
- **No insurance** — ask for confirmation before they start

---

## Tips to Get the Best Deal

- **Bundle jobs**: If you need new power points and a smoke alarm check, do both in one visit — you'll only pay one call-out fee
- **Get 3 quotes**: Prices can vary by $300–$1,000 for the same work
- **Be specific**: The more detail you give in your job description, the more accurate the quote
- **Check reviews**: Look for electricians with consistent, verified reviews from local customers
