## Moving House in New Zealand: Complete Checklist and Costs

Moving house is consistently rated as one of life's most stressful events — but it doesn't have to be. With the right planning and the right removalists, a house move in NZ can be surprisingly smooth. Here's everything you need to know about costs, timelines, and what to do when.

## Removalist Costs in NZ (2025)

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| 2-person crew + truck (per hour) | $100 – $180/hr |
| 3-bedroom house move (local) | $600 – $1,500 |
| 1-bedroom apartment move | $300 – $700 |
| Inter-city move (Auckland to Wellington) | $1,500 – $4,000+ |
| Piano moving surcharge | $200 – $500 |
| Packing service (full home) | $400 – $1,200 |
| Storage (per week) | $80 – $200 |
| Transit insurance (per move) | $80 – $300 |

Local moves (within the same city) are charged by the hour; inter-city moves are typically quoted as a fixed price based on volume and distance.

## The 8-Week Moving Countdown

### 8 Weeks Before Moving Day
- **Book your removalists** — good companies get booked out, especially in December/January and at end of month
- Get at least three quotes; ask about insurance and what happens if items are damaged
- Confirm your moving date with your real estate agent or property manager
- Start researching storage options if there's a gap between settlement dates

### 6 Weeks Before Moving Day
- **Sort, donate, and sell** — moving is the perfect time to cull. Less to move = lower cost
- List unwanted furniture on Facebook Marketplace or TradeMe
- Donate to local op shops (many offer free pickup for larger items)
- Book a skip bin if needed for rubbish

### 4 Weeks Before Moving Day
- **Start packing non-essential items** — books, seasonal clothes, décor, spare linen
- Label every box with contents AND destination room at the new house
- Photograph the backs of entertainment systems before dismantling
- Confirm dates and times with your removalist

### 2 Weeks Before Moving Day
- **Change your address** — NZ Post offers a mail redirection service (from $30 for 3 months — worth doing)
- Update your address with:
  - **IRD** (ird.govt.nz — My IR)
  - **Banks and credit cards**
  - **NZTA** (driver licence address)
  - **Electoral Commission** (enrol.govt.nz)
  - Utilities: electricity, gas, internet, water
  - Subscriptions, insurance policies, doctor/dentist
- Arrange connection of utilities at your new address

### 1 Week Before Moving Day
- Pack everything except daily essentials
- Set aside a "moving day box" — things you'll need immediate access to (kettle, mugs, toilet paper, phone charger, change of clothes, kids' items)
- Confirm access arrangements for both properties (keys, gate codes, parking)
- Defrost the freezer

### Moving Day
- **Clear access at both ends** — ensure removalists have clear paths from truck to door
- Be present or have a trusted person at both properties if possible
- Do a final walkthrough of the old property before handing over keys
- Check the meter readings at both properties and photograph them

### After Moving Day
- Check that everything arrived and nothing is damaged — report issues to removalists within the timeframe on their contract (usually 24–48 hours)
- Unpack essentials first; take your time with everything else
- Introduce yourself to the neighbours

## Transit Insurance: Don't Skip It

Standard removalist contracts in NZ often limit liability to a low per-item maximum (sometimes as little as $100/item). Your household contents insurance may cover transit — check with your insurer first.

If not covered, **transit insurance from the removalist** typically costs $80–$300 and covers full replacement value of your possessions in transit. For a full household move, this is money well spent.

## Tips for Saving Money on Your Move

- **Move mid-week** — Tuesdays to Thursdays are often 10–20% cheaper than weekend moves
- **Move mid-month** — end-of-month demand is highest; prices rise accordingly
- **Pack yourself** — professional packing adds $400–$1,200; doing it yourself saves significant money
- **Disassemble furniture yourself** — beds, dining tables, and flat-pack items can be prepped before the removalists arrive, reducing hourly time
- **Book early** — last-minute bookings carry a premium, especially in summer
