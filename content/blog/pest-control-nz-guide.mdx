# Pest Control NZ: When to Call a Professional

Most New Zealand homeowners will encounter a pest problem at some point — whether it's a wasp nest in the eaves, mice in the kitchen, or cockroaches making an unwelcome appearance. Knowing when to handle it yourself and when to call a professional can save you time, money, and a lot of frustration.

---

## Common Pests in New Zealand Homes

New Zealand has a number of pests that regularly cause problems for homeowners:

- **Wasps** — German and common wasps are a serious problem, particularly in the South Island. Nests can grow to 10,000+ workers by summer
- **Rodents** (rats and mice) — especially during autumn as temperatures drop
- **Cockroaches** — the German cockroach is common in kitchens; the American cockroach in drains and sewers
- **Fleas** — especially in homes with pets
- **Ants** — garden ants are a nuisance; white-footed house ants are harder to eliminate
- **Spiders** — most are harmless, but white-tailed and redback spiders can cause bites
- **Bed bugs** — increasingly common, particularly after international travel

---

## When Can You Handle It Yourself?

For minor, localised pest issues, DIY products from hardware stores are sometimes effective:

- **Single ant trails** — bait traps work well for contained problems
- **Occasional mice** — snap traps are highly effective if you catch the problem early
- **Small wasp nests** (under 20cm, accessible) — can be treated with aerosol at dusk when wasps are inactive

However, DIY solutions often address the symptoms rather than the source. If the problem returns within a few weeks, it's time to call a professional.

---

## When to Call a Professional Pest Controller

You should contact a professional pest control company when:

- **The nest or infestation is large** — large wasp nests, large rodent populations, or widespread cockroach infestations require professional treatment
- **You can't locate the source** — professionals have equipment and expertise to find hidden nests and entry points
- **DIY has failed twice** — repeated treatments that don't resolve the problem indicate a deeper issue
- **The pest poses a health or safety risk** — wasps, rodents (hantavirus), and cockroaches all pose health risks that warrant professional treatment
- **It's a rental property** — under the Residential Tenancies Act, landlords have obligations to provide a pest-free home

## How to Choose a Pest Control Company

When selecting a pest controller in New Zealand:

1. **Check they're a member of PMANZ** — the Pest Management Association of New Zealand sets professional standards
2. **Ask about the products used** — reputable companies use TGA/EPA-approved products and will advise on safety precautions
3. **Get a written quote** — including what's included and any follow-up visits
4. **Ask about guarantees** — many companies offer a return visit if the problem persists within 30–90 days
5. **Check reviews** — look for consistent local reviews, not just national franchises

---

## Prevention Tips

The best pest control is prevention:

- Seal gaps around pipes, windows, and door frames
- Store food in airtight containers
- Fix leaking taps and drains — moisture attracts cockroaches and rodents
- Keep firewood away from the house — it harbours rodents and insects
- Check second-hand furniture and clothing for bed bugs before bringing indoors
