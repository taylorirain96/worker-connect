## Licensing Requirements for NZ Plumbers

In New Zealand, **all plumbing work on sanitary and water supply systems must be done by a licensed plumber** — it's illegal for unlicensed people to do this work. This is governed by the Plumbers, Gasfitters and Drainlayers Act.

You can check that your plumber is licensed on the [Plumbers, Gasfitters and Drainlayers Board website](https://www.pgdb.co.nz/). Always do this before hiring.

There are three main licence types:
- **Certifying Plumber** — can certify their own work (most common)
- **Licensed Plumber** — can do the work but needs a certifying plumber to sign off
- **Tradesperson** — works under supervision

For most residential jobs, you want a **Certifying Plumber**.

## Common Plumbing Issues in Christchurch

**Older pipe systems:** Pre-1980s homes in suburbs like Merivale, Cashmere, and Avonhead often have **copper or galvanised steel pipes** that corrode over time. Symptoms include discoloured water, reduced pressure, and pinhole leaks. Repiping a whole house typically costs $8,000–$20,000+.

**Post-earthquake ground movement:** The 2010–2011 earthquakes caused ground settlement that damaged drainage systems across greater Christchurch. If you're in a liquefaction-affected area (many eastern suburbs), drainage issues may still be appearing years later.

**New build suburbs:** Halswell, Rolleston, and Wigram have seen rapid development. These homes are generally newer with modern fittings, but quick builds sometimes mean shortcuts — post-warranty issues are common.

**Hard water:** Canterbury water is relatively hard. Scale builds up in hot water systems and reduces efficiency over time. Annual hot water cylinder checks are worthwhile.
