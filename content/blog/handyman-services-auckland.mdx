Auckland's housing stock is one of the most diverse in NZ — from character villas in Ponsonby and Grey Lynn to 1970s brick-and-tile in Manurewa, and new townhouses in Māngere and Flat Bush. Older homes in particular generate a constant stream of maintenance and repair work that doesn't fit neatly into any single trade — and that's exactly where a good handyman earns their keep.

## What a Handyman Can (and Can't) Do

**A handyman can typically do:**
- General maintenance and repairs
- Flat-pack furniture assembly
- TV and picture mounting
- Minor carpentry (shelving, skirting, decks)
- Fence and gate repairs
- Painting and minor plastering
- Gutter cleaning and minor roof tile adjustments
- Door and window adjustments
- Basic tiling work (splashbacks, grouting)
- Appliance installation (dishwashers, washing machines)

**A handyman generally cannot do:**
- **Mains electrical work** — requires a Registered Electrical Worker (REW)
- **Plumbing drainage or water supply** — requires a licensed plumber
- **Gas work** — requires a gasfitter
- **Structural building work** — requires an LBP (Licensed Building Practitioner)

A good handyman will be upfront about these limits and tell you when you need a licensed trade. Be wary of one who claims to do everything.

## Auckland Suburbs With High Handyman Demand

Character homes in **Mt Eden, Grey Lynn, Ponsonby, Remuera, and Epsom** generate a lot of handyman work — older weatherboards, sash windows, timber decks, and period detailing all need regular attention. In newer suburbs like **Flat Bush, Hobsonville, and Papakura**, handymen are in demand for post-build maintenance, fence installation, and fit-out work.

## Questions to Ask a Handyman

Before booking:

1. **Are you insured?** — Public liability insurance protects you if something is accidentally damaged
2. **Do you supply your own tools?** — Quality handymen arrive fully equipped
3. **Can you do multiple jobs in one visit?** — Maximise your spend by batching repairs
4. **Can you provide a quote for each task?** — For complex or large jobs, get an itemised quote in writing
5. **Do you have references or reviews?** — Check Google, Neighbourly, or Facebook

## Tips for Getting the Most from a Handyman Visit

- **Write a list before they arrive** — group related jobs together to minimise travel time between tasks
- **Have materials ready** — if you've already bought the flat-pack furniture, screws, or paint, you save time and potentially money
- **Start with the most important job** — if time runs short, the must-haves are done
- **Book a full day for large lists** — full-day rates offer better value per hour than hourly billing
