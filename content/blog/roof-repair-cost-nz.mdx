## Roof Repair Costs in New Zealand

Your roof takes the full force of the NZ weather — from Northland's tropical downpours to Southland's biting southerlies. When something goes wrong, the cost depends heavily on what type of roof you have, the extent of the damage, and how quickly you act.

## Roof Repair Price Guide (2025)

| Repair Type | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Minor leak repair (1–2 points) | $300 – $800 |
| Re-pointing / re-bedding tiles | $500 – $1,500 |
| Replace broken/slipped tiles | $400 – $1,200 |
| Gutter replacement (per metre) | $50 – $80/m |
| Full iron re-roof (per m²) | $80 – $120/m² |
| Full tile re-roof (per m²) | $100 – $160/m² |
| Flat roof membrane repair | $600 – $3,000+ |
| Flashing repair | $300 – $800 |

A standard 150m² iron re-roof typically costs **$12,000 – $18,000** supply and install. Getting multiple quotes is essential as prices vary significantly.

## Common Roof Types in NZ

**Long-run steel (iron):** The most common roof type in NZ — durable, relatively affordable, and handles NZ weather well. Profiles include Corrugate, Trimdek, and Colorsteel.

**Concrete tiles:** Common in Auckland and Hamilton homes from the 1970s–90s. They're heavy and can crack or slip over time. Re-bedding and re-pointing are the main repairs required.

**Clay tiles (terracotta):** Less common but found in older heritage homes. More fragile than concrete; replacement tiles can be hard to source.

**Flat membrane roofs:** Common on commercial buildings and modern residential extensions. Ponding water is the #1 problem — regular inspection is critical.

## When to Repair vs Replace

**Repair** is the right call when:
- Damage is isolated (a few tiles, one leak point)
- The roof is less than 15–20 years old
- The overall structure is sound

**Replace** is often better value when:
- The roof is 25–40+ years old and showing widespread deterioration
- You're paying for repeated repairs on the same sections
- You're doing a major renovation that includes the roofline
- Insurance requires a like-for-like replacement after a weather event

As a rule of thumb: if repair costs exceed 40–50% of a new roof price, replacement is usually more economical long-term.

## Warning Signs of Roof Damage

Look out for these signs that your roof needs attention:

- **Water stains on ceilings** — the leak may be far from where water appears
- **Damp or mouldy smell in roof space** — slow leaks accumulate over time
- **Missing, cracked or slipped tiles** — visible from the ground with binoculars
- **Sagging gutters or rusted downpipes** — water backing up causes structural damage
- **Granules in gutters** (from asphalt shingles) — sign of significant weathering
- **Daylight visible in roof space** — any gap is a water entry point

## Why DIY Roof Repair Is Dangerous

Working at height on a pitched, often slippery roof surface is one of the most dangerous DIY tasks in NZ. Falls from ladders and roofs are a leading cause of serious injuries in home settings. Even experienced tradespeople use fall-arrest systems and scaffolding.

**Always hire a qualified roofer** — not just for safety, but because improper repairs can void insurance claims and cause further water damage.

## Getting a Roof Repair Quote

1. **Describe the symptom** — where you see the leak, what the roof type is, approximate age
2. **Send photos** — a good roofer will often give a ballpark estimate from photos before visiting
3. **Get at least two quotes** — prices vary widely, especially for full re-roofs
4. **Check their work warranty** — reputable roofers offer at least a 5-year workmanship warranty
