# Concrete Driveway Cost NZ: What to Budget in 2026

A concrete driveway is one of the most valuable home improvements you can make in New Zealand. It improves kerb appeal, lasts 30+ years with minimal maintenance, and handles NZ's wet winters far better than gravel or asphalt alternatives. Here's a complete breakdown of concrete driveway costs in NZ for 2026.

## Average Concrete Driveway Costs NZ

| Driveway Size | Typical Total Cost |
|---|---|
| Single car (20–30m²) | $4,000–$7,000 |
| Double car (40–55m²) | $7,500–$13,000 |
| Large (60–80m²) | $12,000–$20,000 |

*Prices include concrete supply, forming, reinforcing, pour, and finishing. Excavation and base preparation extra if required.*

## Price Per Square Metre

Concrete driveways in New Zealand typically cost **$150–$280 per m²** for standard supply and installation. This range covers:

- **$150–$190/m²**: Basic broom-finished concrete, simple flat site
- **$190–$240/m²**: Standard exposed aggregate or coloured concrete, average site
- **$240–$280/m²**: Stamped, decorative, or sloped site with significant prep work

## What Affects Concrete Driveway Cost?

### 1. Concrete Finish

The finish significantly affects price:

| Finish Type | Relative Cost |
|---|---|
| Plain broom finish | Base price |
| Exposed aggregate | +$20–$40/m² |
| Coloured / oxide | +$15–$30/m² |
| Stamped / patterned | +$30–$60/m² |
| Spray-on texture coating | +$25–$45/m² |

Exposed aggregate (where the pebble surface is revealed) is extremely popular in NZ — it's slip-resistant and low-maintenance.

### 2. Site Preparation

If your existing driveway needs to be broken up and removed, or if significant earthworks are needed:
- **Demolition of old concrete**: $80–$150 per m²
- **Excavation and gravel base**: $50–$120/m² depending on depth
- **Retaining or drainage work**: Varies significantly

### 3. Slope and Access

Steep driveways require special detailing to prevent slippage and handle water runoff. Expect to add 15–30% for sloped sites.

### 4. Reinforcing

All driveways should include reinforcing mesh or fibremesh to prevent cracking. This is standard, but check with your concreter that it's included in the quote.

### 5. Thickness

Standard residential driveways are 100mm thick. Increase to 125–150mm for heavier vehicles or commercial use.

### 6. Region

Labour rates vary across NZ. Auckland driveways typically cost 15–25% more than the same job in smaller regional centres.

## Concrete vs Asphalt vs Pavers: Which Is Best for NZ?

| Feature | Concrete | Asphalt | Pavers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lifespan | 30–50 years | 15–25 years | 30–50 years |
| Maintenance | Very low | Resealing every 3–5 years | Occasional re-levelling |
| Appearance | Clean, modern | Black, basic | Premium, varied |
| Cost | $$$ | $$ | $$$$ |
| Best for NZ climate | ✅ Excellent | ✅ Good | ✅ Good |

Concrete is the most popular choice in NZ because of its longevity and low maintenance — especially important given NZ's heavy rainfall and freeze-thaw cycles in alpine regions.

## Do You Need a Consent for a Concrete Driveway?

In most cases, **no**. A standard residential driveway replacement does not require a building consent. However:

- **New vehicle crossing** (connecting to a public road) usually requires approval from your local council or NZTA
- **Stormwater drainage** changes may need approval if you're changing how water flows on your property
- **Heritage or special character zones** may have restrictions — check with your council

Your concreter should be familiar with local requirements and advise accordingly.

## How Long Does a Concrete Driveway Take?

| Stage | Timeframe |
|---|---|
| Site prep and forming | 1–2 days |
| Pour and finish | 1 day |
| Initial cure (foot traffic) | 24–48 hours |
| Vehicle traffic | 7 days minimum |
| Full strength | 28 days |

Weather matters significantly — concrete shouldn't be poured in rain or extreme heat. Your concreter will monitor conditions and schedule accordingly.
