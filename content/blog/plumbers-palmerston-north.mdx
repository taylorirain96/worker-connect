## Why Licensing Matters

All plumbing, gasfitting, and drainlaying work in New Zealand must be carried out by a **registered and licensed practitioner** under the Plumbers, Gasfitters and Drainlayers Act 2006. Before any work starts, ask for your plumber's registration number and verify it at [pgdb.co.nz](https://www.pgdb.co.nz).

Hiring an unregistered tradesperson can:

- Void your home insurance
- Create liability issues when you sell your property
- Lead to costly remedial work if the job isn't done to code

## What to Ask a Plumber Before They Start

1. **What is your PGDB registration number?** — Verify it at pgdb.co.nz
2. **Do you carry public liability insurance?** — Protects you if anything goes wrong
3. **Will you provide a written quote?** — Get it in writing before work starts
4. **Can you provide a compliance certificate?** — Required for notifiable work like gas or sanitary installations
5. **What is your availability?** — Emergency plumbers may charge a premium

## Red Flags to Watch For

- **No registration number or won't provide one** — walk away
- **Pressure to start immediately without a written quote** — legitimate plumbers always quote first
- **Cash-only with no invoice** — NZ plumbers must issue GST invoices
- **Unusually cheap quotes** — may indicate unlicensed work or cut corners

---

## Tips to Get the Best Deal

- **Bundle jobs**: If you need a leaking tap fixed and a hot-water cylinder check, do both in one visit to save on call-out fees
- **Be specific**: The more detail you provide, the more accurate the quote
- **Plan ahead**: Emergency call-outs cost more — book non-urgent work during business hours
- **Compare quotes**: Prices can vary by $200–$800 for the same job
