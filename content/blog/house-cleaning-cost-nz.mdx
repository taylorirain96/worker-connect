## How Much Does House Cleaning Cost in NZ?

Whether you need a weekly tidy, a deep clean before selling, or an end-of-tenancy scrub, knowing what to expect in 2025 helps you budget — and avoid being overcharged.

## House Cleaning Price Guide (2025)

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Regular clean (3-bedroom home) | $120 – $180 |
| Deep clean (3-bedroom home) | $250 – $400 |
| End-of-tenancy clean | $300 – $600 |
| Carpet steam cleaning (per room) | $80 – $150 |
| Window cleaning (exterior + interior) | $150 – $350 |
| Oven clean (standalone service) | $80 – $150 |
| Move-in clean | $200 – $450 |

Prices vary by region — Auckland and Wellington tend to be at the higher end, while Christchurch and smaller cities are often 10–20% cheaper.

## What's Included in Each Type of Clean?

**Regular clean:** Vacuuming, mopping, bathroom wipe-down, kitchen benches, dusting surfaces. Designed to maintain a tidy home on a weekly or fortnightly basis.

**Deep clean:** Everything in a regular clean, plus inside appliances, behind furniture, skirting boards, light switches, door frames, inside cupboards, and thorough bathroom/toilet scrubbing. Takes 4–8 hours for a typical home.

**End-of-tenancy clean:** The most thorough clean — required to meet bond requirements. Usually includes oven clean, carpet steam, window cleaning, and all internal surfaces. Landlords and property managers typically require a professional receipt.

## Hourly vs Fixed-Price Rates

Most NZ house cleaners charge either:

- **Hourly rate:** $35–$60/hr for independent cleaners; $45–$75/hr for cleaning companies
- **Fixed price per job:** More common for deep cleans and end-of-tenancy cleans where scope is agreed upfront

For regular weekly or fortnightly cleans, many cleaners will quote a fixed price once they've assessed your home. This works well for both parties.

## Tips to Reduce Your Cleaning Costs

- **Declutter before the cleaner arrives** — tidying isn't cleaning; having clear surfaces means the cleaner can work faster
- **Book regular cleanings** — most cleaners discount for ongoing bookings vs. one-offs
- **Bundle services** — combining carpet steam and end-of-tenancy clean is usually cheaper than separate bookings
- **Compare quotes** — prices vary widely; getting 2–3 quotes takes minutes and can save $100+

## What to Look for in a House Cleaner

When hiring a cleaner in NZ, look for:

- **Insurance** — check they have public liability insurance in case of breakages
- **References or reviews** — ask for references or check Google/Facebook reviews
- **Supplies their own products** — quality cleaners bring their own equipment and chemicals
- **Police check** — for cleaners entering your home unsupervised, many reputable services offer this
- **Consistency** — for regular cleans, the same cleaner each visit is worth requesting

**Red flags:** Cash only (no receipt), no insurance, unwilling to provide references, quote significantly lower than all others without explanation.
