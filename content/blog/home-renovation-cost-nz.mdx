## How Much Does a Home Renovation Cost in NZ? (2025)

Home renovations in New Zealand can range from a $10,000 bathroom refresh to a $500,000+ full-home transformation. The wide range reflects the huge variation in scope, materials, location, and whether the work requires consents. Here's a practical guide to what different renovation types actually cost.

## Kitchen Renovation Costs

The kitchen is typically the most expensive room to renovate — and often delivers the best return.

| Kitchen Scope | Typical Cost Range |
|---|---|
| Basic refresh (new benchtops, paint, hardware) | $5,000 – $15,000 |
| Mid-range full kitchen reno | $20,000 – $50,000 |
| High-end kitchen with custom cabinetry | $50,000 – $120,000+ |

Factors that affect cost: layout changes (moving plumbing adds significantly), appliance quality, cabinetry material (flat-pack vs custom), and benchtop material (laminate vs engineered stone vs timber).

## Bathroom Renovation Costs

| Bathroom Scope | Typical Cost Range |
|---|---|
| Basic bathroom update (fixtures, tiling) | $8,000 – $20,000 |
| Full bathroom renovation | $18,000 – $45,000 |
| Ensuite (compact) | $12,000 – $30,000 |
| Luxury master bathroom | $45,000 – $100,000+ |

Plumbing relocation, underfloor heating, and custom tiling all increase costs substantially.

## Whole-Home Renovation Costs

| Project Type | Typical Cost Range |
|---|---|
| Interior repaint (3-bed house) | $4,000 – $10,000 |
| New flooring throughout (3-bed house) | $8,000 – $25,000 |
| Partial renovation (kitchen + bathroom) | $35,000 – $100,000 |
| Full renovation (kitchen, bathrooms, living) | $100,000 – $300,000+ |
| Full gut renovation with structural work | $250,000 – $600,000+ |

## Do You Need a Building Consent?

Most structural and significant renovation work requires a **building consent** from your local council. This includes:

- Removing or moving walls (especially load-bearing walls)
- Plumbing relocation
- Adding new rooms or extensions
- Altering the roofline

Consents typically cost **$1,500 – $8,000+** depending on the council and scope of work. Your builder or project manager will usually handle the consent application.

## What Drives Renovation Costs Up

**Unexpected discoveries:** Rotten framing, asbestos, poor wiring, or mould behind walls are common in older NZ homes — and all add to the final bill.

**Heritage features:** Villas and bungalows from the early 1900s require more specialist work to maintain original character features.

**Remote locations:** Tradespeople travelling to rural areas or islands charge for travel time and accommodation.

**Material prices:** Timber, plasterboard, and imported fixtures have increased significantly in price since 2021.

## Tips to Get the Best Outcome

1. **Use a licensed builder for structural work** — LBP certification is required for restricted building work in NZ
2. **Get a fixed-price contract** — cost-plus contracts are risky on older homes with unknowns
3. **Budget a 15–20% contingency** — unexpected discoveries are common
4. **Stage your renovation** — doing it in phases is easier on cash flow and allows you to live in the home
5. **Get at least three quotes** — renovation pricing varies widely between builders
