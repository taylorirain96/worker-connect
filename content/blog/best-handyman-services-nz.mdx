## How to Hire a Reliable Handyman in New Zealand

A good handyman is one of the most useful tradespeople you can have on speed dial. From flat-pack assembly to fence repairs, a skilled handyman can tackle dozens of small jobs that don't quite justify calling a specialist — and at a much lower hourly rate.

## What Can a Handyman Do?

A handyman can generally help with:

- Flat-pack furniture assembly (IKEA, The Warehouse, etc.)
- Minor carpentry repairs — doors, skirting boards, decking boards
- Fence repairs and gate adjustments
- Gutter clearing and minor gutter repairs
- Painting touch-ups and small painting jobs
- Bathroom caulking and silicone removal
- Shelving installation and picture hanging
- Weatherboard replacement (cosmetic repairs)
- Window and door hardware adjustments
- Basic yard tidying and maintenance

**What a handyman usually can't do:** Licensed electrical work, gas fitting, structural plumbing, or work requiring a building consent. For these, you need a licensed tradesperson.

## Typical Handyman Rates in NZ

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Hourly rate | $60 – $120/hr |
| Half-day rate (4 hrs) | $220 – $420 |
| Full-day rate (8 hrs) | $400 – $700 |
| Flat-pack assembly (per item) | $60 – $150 |
| Gutter clean (standard home) | $150 – $300 |
| Fence panel repair | $150 – $400 |

Minimum call-out times are common — most handymen have a 1–2 hour minimum. Materials are usually charged at cost plus a small margin.

## How to Find a Vetted Handyman

**1. Use a platform with verified reviews** — word of mouth is great, but verified reviews from real customers tell you much more about reliability and quality.

**2. Check their range** — describe all your jobs upfront so the handyman can let you know if anything is outside their skill set.

**3. Ask for a written estimate** — for anything more than a 2-hour job, a rough written estimate prevents disputes later.

**4. Combine jobs** — if you have multiple small tasks, group them into a single visit to minimise minimum call-out costs.

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Unable to show any past work or references
- No clear pricing or refusal to estimate
- Asks for full payment upfront
- Doesn't have basic tools of trade
