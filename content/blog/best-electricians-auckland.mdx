# How to Find a Licensed Electrician in Auckland (2025 Guide)

Electrical work is one area where cutting corners isn't just expensive — it can be deadly. In New Zealand, the law is clear: most electrical work must be carried out by a **Registered Electrical Worker (REW)**. Here's what you need to know before hiring an electrician in Auckland, including how to check credentials, what common jobs cost, and the red flags to avoid.

---

## Why Licensing Matters in New Zealand

Unlike some countries where a skilled handyman can do basic wiring, New Zealand law requires that **all electrical work** (beyond changing lightbulbs and resetting circuit breakers) be performed by a registered electrical worker.

The key reasons:

- **Safety** — Faulty wiring is one of the leading causes of house fires in NZ.
- **Insurance** — Unlicensed electrical work can void your home insurance policy.
- **Compliance** — All electrical work must be inspected and certified. Uncertified work must be disclosed when you sell your home.

---

## What Is a Registered Electrical Worker (REW)?

A Registered Electrical Worker is licensed by the **Electrical Workers Registration Board (EWRB)**. There are several categories:

- **Electrician** — handles general residential and commercial wiring
- **Line Mechanic** — works on overhead and underground power lines
- **Cable Jointer** — specialises in underground cable work

For most home and business jobs, you want a **registered electrician**. You can verify any electrician's registration at [ewrb.govt.nz](https://www.ewrb.govt.nz).

---

## How to Check Credentials

Before any work begins, ask your electrician:

1. **Are you a Registered Electrical Worker?** — Ask for their REW number.
2. **Are you a member of the Master Electricians association?** — Not required, but a good sign of professionalism.
3. **Will you provide an Electrical Safety Certificate?** — This is a legal requirement for notifiable work.
4. **Do you have public liability insurance?** — Protects you if something goes wrong.

## Red Flags to Watch Out For

- **No REW number** — Never hire an electrician who can't provide their registration number.
- **Cash only, no receipt** — Legitimate electricians provide invoices and accept electronic payment.
- **Won't provide a safety certificate** — This is a legal requirement. If they won't, walk away.
- **Prices that seem too good to be true** — Cheap unlicensed work costs more in the long run.
- **No insurance** — Always confirm public liability coverage before work starts.
