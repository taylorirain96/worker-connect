## How Much Does a Deck Cost in New Zealand? (2025)

A timber deck is one of the best investments you can make in a New Zealand home — it adds usable outdoor space, improves appeal, and can add meaningful value. But costs vary enormously depending on size, material, height, and whether you need a building consent.

## Deck Cost by Material

| Material | Cost per m² (supply & install) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Treated pine | $500 – $900/m² | Most affordable; requires maintenance every 3–5 years |
| Kwila (hardwood) | $700 – $1,200/m² | Beautiful grain; naturally durable; sustainably sourced |
| Composite decking | $800 – $1,400/m² | Low maintenance; doesn't warp or splinter |
| Cedar | $700 – $1,100/m² | Naturally resistant to rot; lighter weight |
| Aluminium | $900 – $1,600/m² | Longest lifespan; marine environments |

For a standard **30m² treated pine deck** at ground level, expect to pay **$15,000 – $27,000** fully installed.

## Deck Size Cost Examples

| Deck Size | Treated Pine | Composite |
|---|---|---|
| 15m² (small) | $7,500 – $13,500 | $12,000 – $21,000 |
| 30m² (medium) | $15,000 – $27,000 | $24,000 – $42,000 |
| 50m² (large) | $25,000 – $45,000 | $40,000 – $70,000 |

These are rough estimates including labour, materials, and standard posts/framing.

## Do You Need a Building Consent?

In New Zealand, a deck requires a **building consent** if:

- It's more than 1.5m above the ground
- It's attached to the house

Many ground-level decks don't require consent, but your local council has the final say. A builder will usually handle the consent application — budget around **$500 – $2,000** for consent fees depending on your council.

## What Affects the Price?

**Height:** Elevated decks require more substantial framing, longer posts, and sometimes concrete piles — significantly adding to cost.

**Shape and complexity:** L-shapes, curves, or decks with stairs and balustrades cost more than a simple rectangular deck.

**Subfloor condition:** If the builder encounters rock, poor soil, or tree roots, foundation costs increase.

**Balustrades:** Required for decks over 1m high — typically add **$200 – $500 per lineal metre** depending on material (timber vs glass vs aluminium).

**Access:** Difficult site access (steep sections, narrow side gates) increases labour time.

## Choosing a Deck Builder

Look for a builder who:

- Holds a current **Licensed Building Practitioner (LBP)** licence for structural work
- Can handle the consent process on your behalf
- Provides a written quote specifying materials and scope
- Has recent local examples of deck builds you can inspect
