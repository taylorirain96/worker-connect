## How Much Does a Heat Pump Cost to Install in NZ?

Installing a heat pump is one of the smartest investments you can make for a New Zealand home. They heat more efficiently than electric heaters, cool in summer, and can significantly reduce your power bill year-round. But what does heat pump installation actually cost in 2025?

## Average Heat Pump Installation Costs

| System Type | Supply & Install Cost |
|---|---|
| Single-room (wall-mounted) | $1,200 – $2,500 |
| Multi-head (2–3 rooms) | $3,500 – $6,000 |
| Multi-head (4+ rooms) | $5,500 – $10,000+ |
| Commercial or ducted | $5,000 – $20,000+ |

These prices include the heat pump unit, refrigerant lines, electrical work, and installation labour. Always get at least three quotes — prices vary significantly between brands and installers.

## Top Heat Pump Brands in NZ

New Zealand's most popular heat pump brands are:

- **Mitsubishi Electric** — widely regarded as the gold standard for reliability and efficiency
- **Daikin** — excellent energy efficiency ratings, very popular in NZ homes
- **Fujitsu** — solid mid-range option with good after-sales support
- **Panasonic** — strong performer, often competitively priced
- **Haier** and **Samsung** — budget-friendly options worth considering for smaller rooms

For most NZ homes, Mitsubishi and Daikin dominate the market due to their robustness in varying climates from Northland to Southland.

## What Affects the Price?

**Unit size (kW):** A 2.5kW unit suits a small bedroom; a 7kW+ unit is needed for large open-plan living areas. Bigger units cost more to buy and install.

**Brand:** Premium brands like Mitsubishi or Daikin cost more upfront but often have better energy efficiency (higher star ratings = lower running costs).

**Installation complexity:** A straightforward back-to-back install (unit on an exterior wall with a short refrigerant run) is cheapest. Long refrigerant runs, difficult access, or high-rise installs add cost.

**Electrical work:** If your switchboard needs upgrading or a new circuit is required, expect an additional $300–$800 from an electrician.

**Number of indoor heads:** Multi-head systems sharing one outdoor unit are more cost-effective per room than separate systems.

## The Warmer Kiwi Homes Subsidy

If you own and live in your home, you may be eligible for the **Warmer Kiwi Homes** programme, which provides **up to 80% off** the cost of a heat pump or insulation for eligible homeowners.

To qualify, your home generally needs to be:
- Built before 2000
- Owner-occupied
- In a lower income or high health-need area

Check your eligibility and find approved installers at the [EECA website](https://www.eeca.govt.nz/warmer-kiwi-homes/). This is a genuine subsidy that could reduce a $2,000 installation to under $400 — absolutely worth checking before you pay full price.

## Heat Pumps vs Electric Heaters: Running Costs

A heat pump is **3–5x more efficient** than a standard electric heater. For every 1 kW of electricity consumed, a heat pump produces 3–5 kW of heating. This means:

| Heating Method | Annual Running Cost (typical NZ home) |
|---|---|
| Electric panel heaters | $1,800 – $3,000+ |
| Heat pump (quality brand) | $400 – $900 |
| Gas heating | $900 – $1,800 |

Over 5–10 years, the running cost savings easily outweigh the installation cost.

## Tips for Getting the Best Heat Pump Quotes

1. **Get three quotes** — prices can vary by $500–$1,500 for the same job
2. **Ask about efficiency ratings** — higher COP (Coefficient of Performance) = lower power bills
3. **Check the installer is certified** — look for F-Gas certification and ensure they use licensed electricians
4. **Ask about warranties** — most brands offer 5-year parts warranties; some installers offer extended cover
5. **Ask about servicing** — annual filter cleans and checks extend unit life significantly
