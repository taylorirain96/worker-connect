# How Much Does a Bathroom Renovation Cost in NZ? (2026 Price Guide)

A bathroom renovation is one of the highest-return investments in a New Zealand home. Whether you're refreshing an ageing ensuite or gutting a dated main bathroom, understanding the real costs upfront helps you plan, budget, and avoid nasty surprises.

Here's a complete, honest price breakdown for NZ bathroom renovations in 2026.

---

## Average Bathroom Renovation Costs in NZ

| Renovation Type | Estimated Total Cost |
|---|---|
| Cosmetic refresh (taps, accessories, paint) | $1,500 – $5,000 |
| Mid-range renovation (re-tile, new fixtures) | $12,000 – $25,000 |
| Full gut and rebuild | $20,000 – $45,000+ |
| Ensuite (smaller footprint) | $10,000 – $25,000 |
| Luxury master bathroom | $40,000 – $80,000+ |

These are ballpark figures for a standard NZ bathroom (approximately 5–8m²). Larger bathrooms, complex plumbing changes, and premium fixtures push costs higher.

---

## What's Included in the Price?

A mid-range full bathroom renovation typically includes:

**Demolition and strip-out**
- Remove existing tiles, vanity, toilet, bath/shower
- Approximately $800 – $2,000

**Waterproofing**
- Mandatory under NZ Building Code clause E3 before tiling
- Approximately $500 – $1,500 for a standard bathroom

**Tiling (floor and walls)**
- Labour: $60 – $140/m² for supply and install
- A standard bathroom with 20–30m² of tiling: $3,000 – $8,000

**Plumbing**
- Relocating or replumbing bathroom services: $2,000 – $8,000
- Like-for-like fixture replacement: $800 – $2,500

**Electrical**
- Ventilation fan, lighting upgrade, heated towel rail: $600 – $2,000

**New fixtures and fittings (supply)**
- Vanity: $500 – $3,000+
- Toilet suite: $400 – $1,500
- Shower unit or bath: $800 – $5,000+
- Tapware and accessories: $300 – $2,000+

**Gib work and painting**
- $500 – $2,000 depending on scope

---

## What Drives the Cost Up?

**Moving plumbing:** Shifting the location of the toilet, shower, or vanity requires a registered plumber and can add $2,000 – $8,000 to a job. Wherever possible, keep plumbing in the same positions to control costs.

**Tile selection:** Large format porcelain tiles ($80–$200/m²) cost more to supply and install than standard tiles. Feature tiles on bathroom walls add $30–$60/m² in material cost alone.

**Removing a bath:** Converting a bath to a walk-in shower is a popular renovation in NZ, but it involves waterproofing, additional tiling, a shower frame/glass screen, and drain work.

**Permit fees:** Most bathroom renovations don't require a building consent (unless structural changes are made). However, plumbing work must be carried out by a licensed plumber under the Plumbers, Gasfitters and Drainlayers Act 2006.

**Heritage or character homes:** Older homes (pre-1980) may have complications including asbestos in vinyl floor tiles or textured ceilings, outdated plumbing systems, and unusual dimensions that increase costs.

---

## Cost-Saving Tips

- **Keep plumbing in place:** Staying with the same layout saves $2,000 – $5,000
- **Choose mid-range tiles:** There's a huge quality improvement from budget to mid-range tiles, but diminishing returns beyond that
- **Bundle trades:** Having your plumber, tiler, and electrician co-ordinated by a bathroom renovation specialist avoids costly scheduling gaps
- **Supply your own fixtures:** Sourcing your own vanity, toilet, and tapware from a trade supplier or online retailer can save 20–30% on fixture costs
- **Get three quotes:** Prices vary enormously — always compare at least three quotes before committing

---

## How Long Does a Bathroom Renovation Take?

A typical mid-range bathroom renovation takes **2–4 weeks** from strip-out to completion. Key phases:

1. Strip-out (1–2 days)
2. Plumbing rough-in (1–3 days)
3. Gib/substrate work (1–2 days)
4. Waterproofing and cure time (2–3 days)
5. Tiling (2–4 days depending on size)
6. Fixture installation and plumbing fit-off (1–2 days)
7. Electrical fit-off (half to 1 day)
8. Painting and finishing (1–2 days)
