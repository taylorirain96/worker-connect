# Top Tips for Hiring a Cleaner in New Zealand

A professional cleaner can save you hours every week and keep your home looking its best. But with so many options available — from solo operators to franchise companies — knowing how to choose the right cleaner can feel overwhelming. Here's a practical guide to hiring a cleaner in New Zealand.

---

## What Type of Cleaning Service Do You Need?

Before you start looking, decide what kind of cleaning you need:

- **Regular house cleaning** — fortnightly or weekly visits to maintain your home
- **Deep clean** — a thorough top-to-bottom clean, often for spring cleaning or before moving in
- **End-of-tenancy / bond cleaning** — a comprehensive clean to meet landlord or property manager requirements
- **Office / commercial cleaning** — regular cleaning for businesses, usually done early morning or after hours
- **One-off cleans** — before a special event or after a renovation

Each type has different expectations, timeframes, and costs.

| Service Type | Typical Cost (NZD) |
|---|---|
| Regular house clean (3-bed) | $100 – $200 per visit |
| Hourly rate (standard) | $35 – $65/hr |
| Deep clean (3-bed home) | $300 – $600 |
| End-of-tenancy clean (3-bed) | $250 – $550 |
| Window cleaning (add-on) | $80 – $200 |
| Carpet cleaning (per room) | $50 – $100 |
| Office cleaning | $40 – $70/hr |

Prices vary by region — Auckland and Wellington are typically at the higher end. Franchise companies often charge more than independent cleaners, but may offer greater accountability.

## What to Look for When Hiring a Cleaner

### Experience and References

Ask how long they've been cleaning professionally and whether they can provide references. A cleaner with 2–3 years of experience and verifiable reviews is preferable to someone just starting out, especially for regular bookings.

### Police Check and Background

For someone entering your home regularly, consider asking whether they've undergone a police check. Reputable cleaning companies typically conduct background checks on all staff.

### Insurance

Check whether the cleaner or company holds:

- **Public liability insurance** — covers accidental damage to your property
- **ACC levies** — should be current for independent contractors

### Cleaning Products

Discuss whether the cleaner brings their own products or uses yours. Many cleaners prefer to bring their own (included in the price), while others work with your preferred products — useful if you have allergies or prefer eco-friendly options.

### What's Included

Be specific about what you want cleaned. Confirm whether the quote includes:

- Inside the oven
- Inside the fridge
- Window tracks and sills
- Skirting boards
- Laundry area

Misunderstandings about scope are the most common cause of disputes.

---

## Questions to Ask Before Booking

1. Are you insured? What does your insurance cover?
2. Do you bring your own equipment and products?
3. Will the same person clean each visit?
4. What happens if something is damaged or broken?
5. How much notice do I need to give to cancel or reschedule?
6. Do you offer a satisfaction guarantee?

---

## Tips for a Successful Cleaning Relationship

- **Declutter before each visit** — cleaners clean more effectively when surfaces are clear
- **Communicate clearly** — if something wasn't done to your satisfaction, say so promptly
- **Be consistent** — regular bookings at the same time help cleaners plan their schedule
- **Tip if appropriate** — not expected in NZ, but appreciated for exceptional work
- **Review your cleaner** — positive reviews help trustworthy cleaners grow their business

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- **No insurance** — a serious risk if something is damaged
- **No written agreement or quote** — verbal-only arrangements often lead to disputes
- **Very low rates** — may indicate unlicensed workers or poor quality
- **Reluctance to provide references** — a sign of limited experience or past issues
